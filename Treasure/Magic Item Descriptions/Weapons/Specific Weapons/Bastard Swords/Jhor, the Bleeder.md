# Jhor, the Bleeder
*Weapon (Bastard Sword), Uncommon*

You gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls and damage rolls made with this magic weapon.

When you hit a creature with an attack using this magic weapon, you wound the target. At the start of each of its turns for 1 minute, the target takes 1 Slashing damage for each time you've wounded it and then makes a Constitution saving throw (DC 13). On a failed save, the wound keeps bleeding. On a successful save, the wound stops bleeding.

# Jhor, the Bleeder
*Weapon (Bastard Sword), Very Rare*

You gain a +3 bonus to attack rolls and damage rolls made with this magic weapon.

When you hit a creature with an attack using this magic weapon, you wound the target. At the start of each of its turns for 1 hour, the target takes 1d4 Slashing damage for each time you've wounded it and it is unable to regain Hit Points. It then makes a Constitution saving throw (DC 15). On a failed save, the wound keeps bleeding. On a successful save, the wound stops bleeding.

*Baldur's Gate*

*This blade belonged to Ho Mang, a traveler from Kara-Tur who accepted dubious service at courts throughout Faerûn. Skilled in "persuasion," he would use this sword to perform the "Death Of A Thousand Cuts," an ancient ritual from his homeland involving a lengthy series of small, painful incisions. The properties of the blade prevent blood from clotting, and the victim slowly bleeds to death. The sword was stolen, and its whereabouts have been a mystery until now.*
