# Circle of the Beacon
*Serve as a Light When All Others Go Out*  
Life in coastal cities remains distinct from that in the wilderness or other regions, but it is no less rife with primal power. At the sea’s edge, where the morning mist takes the land and the cries of seagulls and haggard sailors fill the sky, Druids of the Circle of Lanterns go about their task. Their light swells together into a well of spiritual energy, one that guides wayward sea captains home in treacherous conditions. Colloquially known as the “Circle of the Lighthouse,” these Druids wield their primal gifts to protect the innocent and downtrodden, protecting vessels from sea monsters and nautical raiders.
*“There are countless lamps throughout this city, and they guide lost souls safely home like a lighthouse does a sailor. I keep them lit, and that little boy or frightened woman gets home safely.” — Callus Lamorde, Druid of Lanterns*

## Level 3: Warden’s Radiance
You wield firelights to banish shadows and uncover devious plots. While a creature is in Bright Light created by one of your spells or Druid features, you have Advantage on Wisdom (Insight or Perception) checks you make against it.

## Level 3: Mantle of Hearth Fire
As a Magic action, you can expend a use of your Wild Shape to cloak yourself in protective hearth fire, instead of assuming a Beast form. The mantle lasts for 1 minute, and it ends early if you dismiss it (no action required), are Incapacitated, or use this feature again. While your mantle is active, you gain the following benefits.  
***Illumination.*** You shed Bright Light in a 10-foot radius and Dim Light for an additional 10 feet.  
***Lantern Fire.*** When you or an ally takes damage from a creature within the Bright Light of your mantle, you can take a Reaction to deal 1d8 Fire to the attacker.  
***Soothing Light.*** Whenever you or another creature of your choice within the Bright Light of your mantle regains Hit Points, you can grant it Temporary Hit Points equal to 1d8 + your Wisdom modifier (minimum of 1 Hit Point).

## Level 6: Lantern Lighter
Your many hours of silent vigil have granted you the magic required to alight the darkness and the threats that lurk within. You always have the Spiritual Weapon spell prepared, it counts as a Druid spell for you, and it doesn’t count against the number of spells you can prepare each day. When you cast Spiritual Weapon, you can modify the spell so that the weapon you summon takes the form of a flickering mote of light that shines with the warmth of a streetlamp. If you do so, the weapon emits Bright Light in a 15-foot radius and Dim Light for an additional 15 feet, and whenever you hit a creature with an attack roll using the spell, its damage type can be Fire or Radiant (your choice) instead of its normal damage type. When a creature takes damage from this modified casting of Spiritual Weapon, it flares with light and the next attack roll made against it before the end of your next turn has Advantage.  
Once you use this feature to modify a casting of Spiritual Weapon, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest. You can also restore your use of it by expending a level 2+ spell slot (no action required).

## Level 10: Enhanced Mantle
You burn brighter, and the light you emit soothes your allies’ wounds and fears. The effects of your Mantle of Hearth Fire improve in the following ways.  
***Illumination.*** You now shed Bright Light in a 20-foot radius and Dim Light for an additional 20 feet.  
***Lantern Fire.*** The damage increases to 2d8, and the damage can be Fire or Radiant (your choice).  
***Soothing Light.*** When you grant these Temporary Hit Points to a creature, the target has Advantage on the next saving throw it makes while it has those Temporary Hit Points.

## Level 14: Wisp of Light
The city is truly beautiful—thousands, even millions, of individual lights swim together in a confused sea, determined to carve out a decent life. The hopes and dreams and worries and joys all blend together into one font of power that you can tap into. As a Bonus Action, you can expend a use of your Wild Shape to shape-shift into a Tiny wisp of ethereal light, warm with the glow of the city’s energy. While in this form, you retain your game statistics, but you appear as a mote of light no bigger than a lantern flame, and the following rules apply.  
***Armor Class.*** Your base Armor Class equals 13 + your Wisdom modifier.  
***Temporary Hit Points.*** When you assume this form, you gain a number of Temporary Hit Points equal to your Druid level, which vanish when you exit the form.  
***Damage Resistance.*** You have Resistance to Bludgeoning, Piercing, and Slashing damage from nonmagical attacks.  
***Conditions and Saving Throws.*** You have Advantage on Strength, Dexterity, and Constitution saving throws. In addition, you have Immunity to the Grappled, Prone, and Restrained conditions.  
***Mantle of Hearth Fire.*** When you assume this form, you can activate your Mantle of Hearth Fire without expending a use of that feature. When used in this way, the mantle lasts for the duration of the form.  
***Movement.*** You have a Fly Speed of 60 feet and can hover. This is your only method of movement. Additionally, you are incorporeal and can move through occupied spaces (such as creatures and objects) as if they were Difficult Terrain. You take 1d10 Force damage if you end your turn inside an object. If you return to your normal form while inside a creature or object, you are shunted to the nearest unoccupied space, and you take 1d10 Force damage for every 5 feet you travel as a result of exiting that form.  
***Actions.*** You can’t attack or cast spells, and you are unable to physically interact with other creatures.  
***Objects.*** You can’t speak or manipulate objects, and any objects you were carrying or holding can’t be dropped, used, or otherwise interacted with. When you transform, you choose whether your equipment falls in your space or merges into your new form. Equipment that merges with the form has no effect while you’re in that form.  
***Luminous Stride.*** While in this form, you can cast the Tree Stride spell as if you had it prepared without expending a spell slot. When you cast it in this manner, you can use it to instead enter lamps, pyres, torches, or any other light source.  
Your wisp form lasts for 10 minutes, and it ends early if you are Incapacitated, you die, or if you have no Temporary Hit Points granted by the form left. You can also leave the form early as a Bonus Action.  
Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest. You can also restore your use of it by expending a spell slot of 4th level or higher (no action required).
