# Tidecaller
*The Ocean Calls Them To Adventure*  
The perilous seas are ripe for exploration. Be it for treasure, charting, or guiding others at sea, these rangers use the ebb and flow of the tides to help others travel safely across vast oceans and face the dangers that rise from the deep.

## Level 3: Safe Passage
You’re accustomed to the perils of the sea, granting you the following benefits:  
***Swimming.*** You gain a Swim speed equal to your Movement Speed, and while you are underwater, you can hold your breath for up to 1 hour.  
***Navigator’s Blessing.*** You gain Proficiency with Navigator’s Tools. If you are already proficient, you can gain Proficiency in a different tool of your choice.  
***Tiding Strikes.*** When you hit a creature with a Weapon Attack, you can deal an additional 1d4 Cold or Acid damage (your choice when you make the attack). If the target is submerged in water, this damage increases to 1d8. A creature can take this damage only once per turn.  
When you reach Level 11 in this class, this extra damage increases to 1d6, or 1d10 if the target is submerged.

## Level 3: Ride the Tide
You’ve learned to use the ebb and flow of the tides to your advantage. Whenever you place a Hunter’s Mark on an enemy, you can produce one of the following effects for the duration:  
***Low Tide.*** The target’s Movement Speed is reduced by 10 for the duration.  
***High Tide.*** Once per turn, when you hit the marked creature with a Weapon Attack, you can push that creature up to 5 feet horizontally in a direction of your choice.

## Level 3: Tidecaller’s Magic

## Level 7: Wave Instructor
Your knowledge of the wild seas grants you insight into how to teach others to survive it. Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can choose a number of creatures equal to your Wisdom Modifier. The chosen creatures gain a Swim Speed equal to their movement speed, and can hold their breath for up to an hour while underwater. This benefit lasts until you finish another Long Rest.

## Level 7: Crashing Tide
You can create a powerful current around you. As a Bonus Action, you can create a 15-foot Emanation centered on you for 1 minute. While this aura is active, a pool of inch-deep water coalesces around you, and you gain the following benefits:  
**Rising Tide.** Whenever you or another creature makes a Strength or Dexterity Saving Throw while within the Emanation, you can use your Reaction to grant that creature a bonus to that save equal to your Wisdom Modifier.  
**Harsh Current.** A creature affected by you Hunter’s Mark suffers an additional effect based on the Tide applied to it:  
***Low Tide:*** If the creature starts its turn within the Emanation, its movement speed is halved until the start of its next turn.  
***High Tide:*** The distance the creature can be pushed increases to 15 feet if it is within the Emanation.  
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom Modifier. You regain one use when you finish a Short Rest, and all uses when you finish a Long Rest.

## Level 11: Follow the Flow
You can use the movement of strong currents to manipulate positioning. You gain the following benefits:  
***Interposing Wave.*** Whenever a creature hits you with an Attack Roll, you can take a Reaction to force Disadvantage on the attack, potentially causing it to miss. Hit or miss, you can then move up to half your movement speed without provoking opportunity attacks.  
If your Crashing Tide is active, you can use your Reaction to apply this effect to an ally within the Emanation.  
***Moonshift Mark.*** Whenever you use Favored Enemy to apply a Hunter’s Mark, you can either choose two targets, applying a different Tide effect to each, or apply both Tide effects to a singular target.

## Level 15: Tidemaster
You’ve reached the apex of Tidecallers, gaining the following benefits:  
***Improved Crashing Tide.*** The range of your Crashing Tide aura increases to 30 feet. You can now use the Rising Tide feature to grant the bonus to every creature of your choice within the Emanation.  
***Improved Tiding Strikes.*** The damage of your Tiding Strikes increases by one die size against creatures that are Huge or larger.
