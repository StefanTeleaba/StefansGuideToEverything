# Pale Voyage Patron
*A Soul Lined with Seasalt*  
Your pact draws on the deep waters and the dead tides, the realms of the unmoored and the unclaimed. You have bargained with a ghost shipan ancient, crewless vessel that sails beyond every chart and every known current, older than any navy and answerable to nothing. You are its agent on shore. Its aims are not evil in the way of demons or devils; it does not hunger for your corruption or your destruction- instead, it yearns for a captain worthy enough to command it, and will test your mettle every step of the way.

## Level 3: Plunder
*“The ship did not choose you for your scruples. Every voyage ends with a reckoning — what was taken, what was owed, and what the sea kept for itself. You are the ship’s hand in the world, and you collect accordingly.”*  
When you hit a creature with an attack roll while within 60 feet of it, you can trigger one of the following effects. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier, regaining all uses on a Long Rest. If a target succeeds on their saving throw, the use is not expended.  
***Steal.*** The target makes a Strength or Dexterity saving throw (their choice) against your spell save DC. On a failure, one worn or carried object you can see (not armor) is teleported to your Patron’s ship for 1 minute. While plundered, the creature can use an action to make a Charisma check against your spell save DC, recovering the item on a success. If the creature dies while the item is plundered, it appears in the nearest unoccupied space to you.  
***Siphon.*** The target makes a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failure, choose a damage type and apply one of the following based on their resistances:
- If they have resistance to the chosen type, they
lose it and you gain it.
- If they have no resistance or immunity, they
gain vulnerability to it and you gain resistance
to it.

Both effects end simultaneously when the target takes damage of the chosen type.  
When a creature affected by Plunder dies, gold pieces equal to twice your Warlock level appear in the nearest unoccupied space to you.

## Level 6: Part of the Ship, Part of the Crew
*“A captain is only as good as their crew. You have sailed long enough now that the ship recognizes yours — their faces, their names, their worth.”*  
At the end of a Long Rest, you can designate a number of creatures within 60 feet of you to be part of your crew. The total number of creatures you can have is equal to 4 + your Charisma Modifier. You and your First Mate are considered to be part of your crew at all times, and do not count against the total number of creatures you can have within your crew. Creatures in your crew are dubbed as ‘crewmates’ for the sake of this feature.  
Creatures remain in your crew until they die, or you use this feature again.  
While a creature is part of your crew, they have the following benefits:  
***Share in the Spoils:*** When you Plunder a damage resistance, your crew mates retain that damage resistance for as long as you do.  
***Cutthroats of the Crow’s Nest:*** When a crewmate within 30 feet of another crewmate makes an Intimidation or Perception check, they gain a bonus to the check equal to your Charisma Modifier.  
***Articles of Incorporation:*** Crewmates have advantage on Charisma Saving Throws as long as they are within 30 feet of a crewmate.  
***Aye Aye, Captain!:*** When a Crewmate makes a critical strike, you regain an expended use of your Plunder.

## Level 10: Sunken Stratagems
You gain the following Bonus Actions. You can use each of them once per Long Rest, or restore one of their uses by expending a Pact Spell Slot (no action required).  
***Give No Quarter (1/Day).*** Any crewmates within 60 feet of you have advantage on attack rolls until the end of your next turn, and can use a reaction immediately to make one weapon attack or cast a cantrip with a casting time of one action.  
***Hoist the Colors (1/Day).*** You plant a spectral flag with your ship’s colors on it, which remains for one minute. While within 60 feet of the flag, you and your crewmates have advantage on Saving Throws to avoid or end the Charmed or Frightened Condition, and Hostile creatures have disadvantage on saves to avoid or end the Charmed or Frightened Condition.  
***Weigh Anchor (1/Day).*** You target a crewmate within 60 feet. That crewmate receives Temporary Hit Points equal to your Warlock Level, and it can immediately take a reaction to either move up to half its speed without provoking opportunity attacks or remove one of the following conditions affecting it: Grappled, Incapacitated, Paralyzed, Prone, Restrained, or Stunned.

## Level 14: Full Speed Ahead
*“The ship has been watching. It always has. And now, when you call, it comes — sails full of a wind that blows from nowhere, hull cutting through earth and stone as easily as open water. Your patron has arrived.”*  
As an action, you summon your spectral ship beneath your feet. It sails forward at a breakneck pace in a 120-foot long, 20-foot-wide Line from you, and it passes through objects and creatures. You and any of your crewmates within 30 feet of you can leap to the deck as it travels. You teleport to an unoccupied space of your choice in the Line, and any creatures who accompanied you appear in the closest unoccupied spaces to you.  
Each other creature in the line must succeed on a Dexterity Saving Throw against your spells save DC A creature who fails this save takes 4d10 Cold & 4d10 Bludgeoning damage and is pushed up to 10 feet away from the Lines point of origin, and only takes half as much damage on a success.  
Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest unless you expend a Pact Magic spell slot (no action required) to restore your use of it.
