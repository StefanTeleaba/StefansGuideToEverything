# Warrior of the Wandering Blade
[*Created by Spaghetti0*](https://bio.site/spaghetti0)

Attack in Tandem with a Floating Weapon Warriors of the Wandering Blade learn to invest part of their own spirit into non-living objects, granting them powers to learn more about those objects and animate them with spiritual energy. These Monks use this power to bond with weapons, which they telekinetically wield with finesse and grace, forming a double-threat on the battlefield.

## Level 3: Object Affinity
You are able to forge connections with objects to control them and learn more about them. You can cast the Mage Hand and Identify spells without Verbal or Material components, though you can only cast Identify as a Ritual. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for these spells, and their effects are altered in the following ways.  
***Mage Hand.*** When you cast Mage Hand, the spectral hand is invisible, and it is only able to interact with objects.  
***Identify.*** When you cast Identify and target an object, you can spend 1 Focus Point to also learn the last time the object was moved by a creature, how far it was moved, and you receive a mental image of that creature.

## Level 3: Spirit Weapon
Over the course of a Long Rest, you can imbue part of your spirit within a Monk weapon you are holding, thereby making it your Spirit Weapon. The weapon remains your Spirit Weapon until you die, or until you imbue another one.  
***Floating Weapon.*** As a Bonus Action you can expend one Focus Point to throw the Spirit Weapon into the air. When you do, the weapon begins to hover, flies up to 30 feet and attacks one creature you can see within its reach (see Attacks below). The weapon remains floating for 10 minutes, until you have the Incapacitated condition, or until you call it back early (no action required). When the weapon stops floating, it flies toward you, and you can choose to wield it if you have a free hand. Otherwise, it harmlessly falls to the ground.  
***Attacks.*** The weapon uses your bonuses to attack and damage, as if you were wielding it, but you must use your Martial Arts die for the weapon’s damage roll.  
While the Spirit Weapon is floating, whenever you make an attack as part of the Attack action, or as part of your Bonus Action through your Martial Arts or Flurry of Blows features, you can make the attack using the weapon. You can also make Opportunity Attacks through the weapon’s space.  
***Moving the Weapon.*** Once on each of your turns, immediately before you make an attack with it, you can move the weapon up to 30 feet.

## Level 6: Refined Control
Your Spirit Weapon’s movements are improved. You can now move the weapon freely during your turn, instead of only once. The total distance the weapon can move on a turn is equal to your Speed.  
Furthermore, you can now use the weapon as a platform. You can mount or dismount the weapon by entering or leaving its space, costing you no extra movement. You can balance on top of it for as long as it continues floating, though you fall off if you have the Prone condition. While balancing on it, you can spend 10 feet of movement to jump 30 feet, using it like a springboard. The weapon can’t move, make attacks or do anything else while it’s supporting you, and once you dismount it, it still can’t move until the end of that turn.

## Level 11: Improved Spirit Weapon
While your Spirit Weapon is floating, it improves in the following ways.  
***Flanking Strike.*** Once per turn when you make an Unarmed Strike against a creature, you can expend 1 Focus Point to grant yourself Advantage on the attack roll, provided your Spirit Weapon is floating within 5 feet of it. On a hit, the target takes extra damage of the weapon’s type equal to one roll of your Martial Arts die.  
***Spirit Rider.*** While you are balancing on your Spirit Weapon, you can expend your movement to cause the weapon to move, effectively giving yourself a Fly Speed equal to half your Speed.  
When you reach Monk level 17, the Fly Speed increases to be equal to your Speed.

## Level 17: Destructive Trajectory
As a Magic action while your Spirit Weapon is floating, you can expend 5 Focus Points to cause it to fly in a destructive path you describe that is no more than 60 feet long. If you are balancing on the weapon, you move along with it, and do not provoke Opportunity Attacks. Each creature within 5 feet of the weapon’s path must make a Dexterity saving throw. A creature takes 5d12 damage of the weapon’s type on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one.
