# Aegis
[*Created by Joe Bushi*](https://bio.site/spaghetti0)

Aegis are shield masters who have elevated defense into an art form, learning to wield their shields as versatile weapons while becoming unshakeable guardians for their allies. Through dedicated training, they transform shields from simple protective equipment into instruments of both devastating offense and selfless intervention.  
An Aegis excels at reading the battlefield and positioning themselves where they're needed most. They leap to intercept deadly blows, mark enemies for their companions to exploit, and create zones of protection that turn vulnerable allies into formidable combatants. While other warriors chase glory through personal achievement, an Aegis measures success by the allies who survive the battle—and they'll throw themselves into harm's way without hesitation to ensure everyone makes it home.

## Level 3: Shield Arsenal
Through rigorous training, you've learned to wield shields not just as protection, but as devastating weapons in their own right.  
While you are wielding a shield, it is considered a weapon with the following statistics:

| Weapon | Damage          | Properties                      | Mastery |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------------------|---------|
| Shield | 1d6 Bludgeoning | Thrown (20/60), Versatile (1d8) | Push    |

While using a shield as a weapon, it still grants you an AC bonus.

## Level 3: Stand Behind Me
Your instinct to protect others has become legendary. When danger threatens your companions, you move with supernatural speed to place yourself between them and harm.  
As a reaction when a creature you can see within 10 feet of you is targeted by an attack, you can instantly move to an unoccupied space within 5 feet of that creature (this movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks) and become the target of the attack instead, provided your speed isn’t 0. When you do so, you gain temporary hit points equal to 1d10 + your fighter level as your protective instincts steel you against the incoming blow.  
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Constitution Modifier. You regain 1 expended use when you finish a Short Rest and all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.  
The area of effect for Stand Behind me increases to 15 feet at level 10, and to 20 feet at level 18.

## Level 7: Fortified Grip
You've learned to grip your shield in ways that maximize both its offensive power and defensive capabilities, turning it into an extension of your will.  
***Improved Shield Fighting:*** Your shield's damage die increases to 1d8 when wielded in one hand, or 1d10 when wielded in two hands. While you are wielding a shield in two hands or dual wielding shields, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.  
When you dual wield shields, only one shield's AC bonus is added to your AC (in addition to the +1 bonus from this feature), rather than adding both shields' AC bonuses together.  
At 15th level, your shield's damage die increases to 1d10 when wielded in one hand, or 1d12 when wielded in two hands. Additionally, the AC bonus you gain from wielding a shield in two hands or dual wielding shields increases to +2.  
***Shield Recall:*** Whenever you throw a shield as part of the attack action, it immediately returns to you, regardless of whether you hit or miss.

## Level 10: Thunderous Challenge
As a Bonus Action, you can strike your shield, releasing a thunderous boom. Each creature of your choice within 30 feet of you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier) or be taunted by you until the start of your next turn.  
While taunted, a creature has disadvantage on attack rolls against targets other than you until the start of your next turn.  
Once you use this feature, you cannot use it again until you finish a long rest. You can also restore your use of this feature by expending a use of Second Wind (no action required).

## Level 10: Ever Vigilant
When you roll for Initiative and have no uses of Stand Behind Me remaining, you regain one use.

## Level 15: Living Bastion
When an allied creature within range of your Stand Behind Me feature is forced to make a Strength or Dexterity saving throw, you can expend a use of Stand Behind Me to move to a space within 5 feet of them without provoking opportunity attacks, provided your speed isn’t 0. The creature makes the saving throw with advantage, taking no damage if they succeed on the saving throw and only half damage if they fail. If your movement causes you to be subjected to the same effect, you also make your saving throw with advantage, taking no damage if you succeed on the saving throw and only half damage if you fail.

## Level 18: Perfect Protector
Your dedication to your allies transcends mortal limits—you are everywhere at once, an omnipresent guardian who never fails to intercede when danger strikes.  
Allied creatures within range of your Stand Behind Me feature have half cover.  
As a Bonus Action, you can grant an ally within range of your Stand Behind Me feature the effects of the Dodge action until the start of their next turn.
