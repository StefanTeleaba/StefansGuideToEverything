# Fencer
[*Created by the_bluez*](https://bio.site/spaghetti0)

*En garde!*  
Your combat style is precise and for you a clash is a careful and important performance. As such you depend on your ability to wield your weapon to great effects and to control opponents with your wit.

## Level 3: Duelist
Specialized in one-to-one combat, you excel into drawing out single opponents in duels to the death. You gain the following.  
***I Challenge You!*** As a Bonus Action or as part of an Attack action, you initiate a Challenge.  
Choose an enemy within 60 feet of which you are aware. For the next minute, or until one of you becomes Unconscious, the enemy becomes a Contender and gains +2 to attack rolls against you until the Challenge ends.  
You gain a pool of Duel Dice (DD), which are d6s, equal to your Fighter level.  
DD are expended when you use them and you can expend a number equal to your Proficiency Bonus, each turn, until the start of your next turn.  
You can initiate a Challenge once and regain all expended uses at the end of a Short or Long Rest. You can also expend a Second Wind use, no action required, to regain one expended Challenge use.  
***Swordplay.*** While in a Challenge you can use a variety of maneuvers against your Contender:  
**Evasive Maneuver.** When targeted by an attack you can use your Reaction and roll one DD, adding the result to your AC until the start of your next turn.  
**Graceful Finisher.** When you inflict damage with a Melee attack, you can roll DD and add the result to the damage.  
**Lunging Step.** As a Bonus Action you expend one DD and gain Reach with your weapon until the start of your next turn.  
**Precise Strike.** Roll one DD and add the result to your Melee attack roll.  
**Show Off.** As part of a Deception, Intimidation, or Persuasion ability check you can expend a DD and roll it. You add the result to the ability check.  
**Taunting Wit.** When your Contender attacks a creature other than you, you can expend a DD as a Reaction. You roll the die and inflict the result as Psychic damage to the Contender, which has Disadvantage on their next attack.

## Level 3: Gallant Style
You gain the following.  
***Bravado.*** You gain proficiency with Charisma saving throws.  
***Dexterous.*** You gain proficiency in the Sleight of Hand skill. If you are already proficient in it you can choose another Fighter skill instead.  
***Stylish Display.*** While in a Challenge, you can recover expended DD once per round by doing one of the following.  
- You strike an enemy with a Melee attack or Unarmed Strike and recover one DD. You can't expend any Duel Dice as part of this attack.
- You activate Second Wind or expend one of its uses to recover DD equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

## Level 7: Circle of Arms
You gain the following.  
***En Garde!*** You can initiate a Challenge as part of rolling initiative. You can do so twice and you regain one expended use at the end of a Short Rest or all expended use at the end of a Long Rest.  
***Point the Sword.*** You can change your Contender before the minute elapses as a Bonus Action, once per Challenge issued, choosing another enemy within 60 feet of you. You can also do so as a Reaction if your previous Contender becomes Unconscious before the minute elapses.

## Level 10: Jack of Arms
You gain an additional use of your Challenge feature.  
You can expend up to two DD when using the Evasive Maneuver, Precise Strike, Show Off, or Taunt features instead of one.

## Level 15: Duelling Elegance
When you roll DD, you can roll one of them twice and take the highest result.  
When using your Jack of Arms feature you can roll each DD twice and take the highest result on each one. You can do so twice and you recover one expended use at the end of a Short Rest, or all expended uses at the end of a Long Rest.

## Level 18: Flower of Battle
You are at the peak of your combat abilities.  
At the start of your turn, while you are in a Challenge, you regain two Duel Dice.  
When rolling initiative, you recover one expended use of Challenge.  
You can expend a number of DD equal to double your proficiency bonus until the start of your next turn.
