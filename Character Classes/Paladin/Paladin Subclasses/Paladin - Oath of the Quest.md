# Oath of the Quest
[*Created by Jezflix*](https://bio.site/spaghetti0)

The Paladins who swear this oath are bound by the legacy of the wanderer. Their tenets emphasize that while a sword can end a life, only a mind can win a journey.  
These paladins share the following tenets:
- Test Your Courage & Overcome Your Fears.
- Help Those Who Put Their Trust in You.
- There Will Always Be a Way Through.
- Wit Before Weaponry.

## Level 3: Oath of the Quest Spells
The magic of your oath ensures you always have certain spells ready; when you reach a Paladin level specified in the Oath of the Quest Spells table, you thereafter always have the listed spells prepared.

## Level 3: Polytropic Touch
Your restorative touch does more than knit flesh; it clears the fog of war and sharpens the resolve of your companions. You can use your Lay on Hands feature in the following new ways:  
***Tactical Clarity.*** As a Bonus Action, you can expend 5 points from your Lay on Hands pool to grant a creature you touch Advantage on the next Ability Check or Attack Roll it makes before the start of your next turn.  
***Pathfinder’s Grace.*** When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you is forced to make a Dexterity saving throw or begins its turn, you can expend 5 points from your Lay on Hands pool to use your Reaction to grant it Advantage on their next Saving Throw (including the one that may have triggered this reaction) and its Speed increases by 10 feet until the end of its next turn.

## Level 3: Cunning Thoughts
You gain proficiency in one of the following skills: History, Insight, Investigation, Perception, or Survival.  
Additionally, as a Magic action, you can expend one use of your Channel Divinity to grant yourself and all creatures you choose within 10ft radius of you a bonus to their next ability check equal to your Charisma modifier (+1 minimum) when using a skill.  
The bonus fades after one hour if not used before.

## Level 7: Aura of Perseverance
Inspiring those around you, both you and your allies can treat their Exhaustion level as one level lower while within your Aura.  
Additionally, while within your Aura, you and your allies are unaffected by Difficult Terrain.

## Level 15: Lessons Learned
At 15th level, your long journey has taught you a multitude of skills. You gain a Climb Speed and Swim Speed equal to your Speed.  
Additionally, whenever you finish a Long Rest, choose two of the following benefits, which last until your next Long Rest:  
***The Beggar’s Guise.*** You can cast Disguise Self at will. You no longer have disadvantage on Stealth checks from Heavy Armor, and you and allies within your Aura gain Advantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks. Additionally, while you are obscured by lightly or heavily obscured terrain (like foliage or fog) or half or greater cover, you can take the Hide action as a Bonus Action.  
***Unbroken Wanderer.*** Your Aura of Perseverance improves to now ignore the effects of the first three levels of exhaustion. Additionally, when you spend Hit Dice during a short rest, you regain the maximum number of HP for each die. Finally, once per long rest, if you drop to 0 hit points but aren’t killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead.  
***Polyglot’s Authority.*** You can cast Comprehend Languages and Tongues at will, without expending a spell slot, but only targeting yourself. Additionally, creatures within your Aura act as if under the effect of a Zone of Truth spell.  
***Strategist’s Foresight.*** You cannot be surprised. You gain a bonus to Initiative equal to your Charisma modifier. Additionally, when you roll Initiative, you can choose one ally within your Aura and you and that ally can swap Initiative results. Once per combat, when a creature you can see makes an attack roll against you, you can use your reaction to impose Disadvantage on that roll.  
***Steel Resolve.*** Choose one damage type: Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, Poison, or Thunder. You and friendly creatures within your Aura have Resistance to that type and are Immune to being Charmed or Frightened. Additionally, whenever a creature within your Aura takes damage of the chosen type, you can use your reaction to cause the attacker to take an equal amount of damage of that same type.

## Level 20: Hero of Legend
You can empower yourself with the legends of your great deeds. As a Bonus Action, you gain the benefits below for 1 minute. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest. You can also restore your use of it by expending a level 5 spell slot (no action required).  
***Guided by the Gods.*** You are blessed with divine foresight. You have Advantage on all attack rolls.  
Additionally, enemies can no longer have Advantage when attacking you.  
***Tested by the Gods.*** If you pass a saving throw and would normally take half damage, you take none instead.  
***Inspired by the Gods.*** You and allies in your Aura are treated as having Proficiency in all skills; if you are already proficient, you grant Expertise.
