# Drowned
*Death is just Deeper Water.*  
Not everything that drowns stays dead. Some return not as ghosts, but as something colder and more deliberate. The Drowned move through the world like a current: patient, purposeful, and impossible to stop. Where they walk, the air tastes of brine and the temperature drops a degree, and creatures with good instincts feel the sudden, irrational urge to look behind them. The sea gave them back for a reason, and they intend to see that reason through — whatever it costs, whoever stands in the way.

## Level 3: Waterlogged
You gain a Swim Speed equal to your Speed, and can breathe underwater.

## Level 3: Cold Waters
The experience of drowning has waterlogged your soul, and the tide is now inextricably part of you. When you take your Cunning Action, you can draw on the power of the sea to modify yourself and the world around you.  
You can modify your Cunning Action in the following ways a number of times equal to your Dexterity Modifier, and regain all uses on a Short or Long Rest.  
If a Cunning Action requires a Saving Throw, the DC equals 8 plus your Dexterity modifier and Proficiency Bonus.  
***Tidal Bore.*** You transform into a Crashing Wave. When you use the Dash action as part of your Cunning Action, you can choose a creature within 5 feet of yourself that is Large or smaller. An unwilling creature must succeed on a Strength Saving Throw. On a failure, you move the creature with you until the end of your turn. If a hostile creature succeeds on the saving throw, your use of Cold Waters is not expended for this turn.  
***Diffluence.*** You take on the formlessness of water. When you use the Disengage action as part of your Cunning Action, you gain the ability to move through other creatures' spaces. A creature whose space you move through has their speed halved until the end of their next turn.  
***Undertow.*** Like water, you can slip through any cracks, and any surface can be the sea. When you use the Hide action as part of your Cunning Action, you gain a Burrow Speed equal to your Swim Speed until the end of your turn. If you end your turn submerged, you float back to the surface, appearing in the space you entered, or the nearest unoccupied space.

## Level 9: Sinking Strike
You gain the following Cunning Strike Options:  
***Fill Their Lungs (Cost: 1d6).*** The target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw, or its lungs fill with water until the start of its next turn. While under this effect, the target cannot speak or cast spells with a Verbal Component, and has disadvantage on Charisma Checks and Saving Throws.  
***Pull Under (Cost: 2d6).*** The target must succeed on a Strength saving throw, or it has the Restrained condition until the end of its next turn. While Restrained in this way, the target has disadvantage on Constitution Saving Throws.

## Level 13: Whirlpool
As an action, you can cause deep water to swirl in a 30-foot emanation around you for 1 minute. When you do so, you can designate creatures to be unaffected by it. Any other creature treats the emanation as difficult terrain, and any creature that starts its turn there must succeed on a Strength saving throw or take 6d6 bludgeoning damage and be pulled 10 feet toward you. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a Long Rest.

## Level 17: Drown in the Depths
When you hit a creature with an attack roll and deal your sneak attack damage, you may forgo your sneak attack dice to transport the creature to a watery grave. The creature is teleported to a watery demiplane, and immediately begins drowning. The creature makes Wisdom saves at the end of each of its turns to end the effect, and the DC equals 8 plus your Dexterity Modifier and your Proficiency Bonus. Once a creature succeeds against this effect, it reappears with the Prone Condition in the space it was last in or the nearest unoccupied space. Once a creature escapes the demiplane, it is immune to this effect for the next 24 hours.
