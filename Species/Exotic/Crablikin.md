# Crablikin
Slowly evolving over thousands of years, Crablikins are mostly solitary explorers and scavengers. They have evolved a humanoid appearance with a crustacean-like exterior and a right hand pincer alongside a humanoid left hand. Crablikin are most often found in coastal areas, riverlands, or swamps.  
**Adroit Explorers**  
Due to their lifestyle Crablikin are natural explorers and travellers. Often scavenging anything they can use or sell, escorting others or joining ship crews to traverse the open ocean. A Crablikin’s need to explore and search is one of the biggest driving factors guiding their life. The joy of discovering lost ruins, valuable salvage or reaching a distant city is the purest fulfillment a Crablikin can feel.  
**Solitary Existence**  
Most Crablikin explore the world alone. When they meet another of their kind they often spend a few years together before parting ways again. Though long lived, they may go decades or centuries without encountering another Crablikin. For them, a few years together is a fleeting moment in the grand scheme of life.

**Crablikin Traits**

**Creature Type:** Humanoid  
**Size:** Medium (about 6—8 feet tall)  
**Speed:** 30ft, swim speed equal to your walking speed

As a Crablikin, you have these special traits.

***Pincer Grip.*** When you use your Unarmed Strike and deal damage, you can choose to lash out with your Pincer. You deal Slashing damage equal to 1d6 plus your Strength modifier instead of the normal damage of an Unarmed Strike. In addition, when a creature is Grappled by your pincer, it has Disadvantage on ability checks it makes to break free.

***Crustacean.*** Your hardened shell grants you Resistance to Bludgeoning damage.

***Hardened Explorer.*** Your solitary, travel heavy lifestyle has honed your survival skills.  
You gain the following benefits:
- *Freedom of Movement.* Your movement ignores non-magical Difficult Terrain.
- *Well Rested.* When you finish a Long Rest, your Exhaustion level reduces by 2 instead of 1, if you have any.
- *Traveller’s Vitality.* When you expend a Hit Point Die to recover Hit Points, you can use the maximum value instead of rolling it.  
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.
