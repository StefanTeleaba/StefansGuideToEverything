# Phocan
Phocans are human-like folk with the thick, oily skin and head of a seal. Native to polar regions of intense cold, they sport a thick layer of blubber, which allows them to survive the harsh weather and the ice-cold waters in which they hunt. Their faces feature large round eyes and sensitive whiskers, which grant them enhanced sight, especially underwater.  
Phocan communities have long since mastered the art of dealing with the cold and have progressed from merely surviving to thriving there. They are renowned for the impressive structures they build out of ice and snow, ranging
from the humble igloo to spectacular palaces built of packed snow. Visitors speak warmly of their hospitality, as well as the games they play, which often feature one Phocan sliding (or sometimes rolling) their rotund bodies in attempts to knock down objects or other Phocans.  
Phocans rarely become adventurers, typically preferring the comforts of their icy homes, but every so often one will seek a life of excitement and discovery. Others find themselves swept up in a grand quest, often in the interest of protecting their homeland.

**Phocan Traits**

**Creature Type:** Humanoid  
**Size:** Medium (about 4-6 feet tall)  
**Speed:** 30 ft.

As a Phocan, you gain the following Special Traits:

***Blubber.*** You have Resistance to Cold damage.

***Deep Senses.*** You have Darkvision with a range of 60 feet. Furthermore, while you are submerged in water, you also have Blindsight with a range of 60 feet, though this sense does not extend beyond the body of water you’re in.

***Hold Breath.*** You can hold your breath for up to 1 hour.

***Swim Speed.*** You have a Swim Speed equal to your Speed.

***Seal Slide.*** As a Bonus Action, you can slide along the ground or dash through water up to 30 feet in a straight line without provoking Opportunity Attacks. Each creature whose space you pass through must make a Dexterity saving throw. The DC is equal to 8 plus your Proficiency Bonus and your Constitution modifier, and creatures that are one or more size categories larger than you have Advantage on their save. A creature that fails its save takes a number of d4s of Bludgeoning damage equal to your Proficiency Bonus and has the Prone condition. On a successful save it takes
half as much damage only.  
You can use this Bonus Action a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.
