# Sharknado
*Level 7 Conjuration (Cleric, Druid, Sorcerer, Wizard)*

**Casting Time:** Action  
**Range:** 120 feet  
**Components:** V, S, M (a badly drawn charcoal or ink sketch of a shark in a tornado)  
**Duration:** Concentration, up to 1 minute

*“So, there was this boy who once really wanted to be a Marine Biologist. Like most boyhood dreams, he grew out of it and went off to magic school like all smart boys do and seemed to excel in one very specific area - making wind blow. Over the years as he grew older, those spells got more powerful until he was able to summon tornados. Very effective, very powerful, but also kinda boring.*  
*At the loss of his mother (his father dying many years earlier), he was cleaning out her belongings (as you do), and found a chest full of drawings he had done as a boy depicting many of the marine creatures he was so interested in. Inspiration happened and the world was forever changed.”*

At a point within range, you summon a 15-foot radius, 60-foot high spherical column of roaring winds and suspiciously displaced apex predators. The area is heavily obscured by a chaotic swirl of seafoam, salt spray, and what appears to be poorly created random pieces of mock debris.

Creatures inside have the Blinded condition in relation to anything outside the tornado, and creatures outside cannot see in (though they can definitely hear the screams of anyone caught inside it).

Each creature that starts its turn in the area or enters it for the first time on a turn must make a Strength saving throw. On a failure: The creature is knocked Prone.  
If a creature starts its turn already Prone within the tornado, it must repeat the Strength save. On a failure, it is sucked 10 feet up into the funnel. It continues to rise 10 feet at the start of each of its turns until it reaches the 60-foot ceiling.

While engulfed within the tornado (off the ground), a creature is Restrained by the centrifugal force and takes 3d10 Piercing damage at the start of each of its turns as the sharks realize they’re just as confused and hungry as
they look.

A creature caught in the funnel can use its Action to make a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check against your Spell Save DC. On a success, they manage to kick off a passing Great White and propel themselves out of the cylinder, landing Prone in the nearest unoccupied space outside the storm.

When the spell ends, the sharks and tornado vanish, and any creatures or objects held aloft plummet to the ground, taking standard fall damage and landing - you guessed it - Prone.

*“What? Sharks in a tornado? How does that work?*  
*Magic!”*
